Our Suppliers

Beef, carabeef, and lamb

We procure our cows, buffalos, and goats from Sunset Farm, Elysian Agro, Argus Farms, and Taurus Fields – some of the finest farms in Bangladesh, ensuring high quality, firm, soft, well marbled meats. The cattle are selected by company representatives and brought to our registered abattoirs for slaughter, where our own butchers, who also then butcher and classify all the meats, for easy transportation back to our well-equipped storehouses. From there, these are sent directly to you, all 100% halal. However, the Top Round, Bottom Round, Lifter and Pectoral muscle groups are separated in the abattoir and sent to smokehouses including Rabbi Smokehouse. Big Smoker, and SmokeItUp. Here, the meats are further prepared, using seasoning powders and rubs, herbs, and a large amount of salt, to draw out all the moisture. Then, the prepared meats are either smoked to make jerky and biltong, or needled thoroughly to make hunter’s beef. The final products are then sent back to us, still 100% halal. Our teas are imported directly from Kashmir, ensuring the finest quality. As for spices, we have contracts with high quality raw spice producers, who make custom spice products just for you, at our behest. We collect paneer from Pure White Farm, who specialize in dairy products and can provide high quality paneer in bulk. We routinely audit their processes as well, to ensure you get the freshest, most delicious paneer.

Our Processes

Butchering

We have our own registered butchers and slaughterhouses. Our representatives regularly conduct health inspections, ensuring your ordered meat is handled in a completely sterile environment.

Curing

Our partners use the highest quality rubs and herbs to impart flavor deep into the meat. The meats are dry brined and cured for varying periods, depending on the desired final product.

Drying

The meats are hung to dry and or smoked, depending on which flavor is planned for at the time. This takes a large amount of time and the process results in most of the water content inside the meat evaporating. This makes the meat much lighter, and concentrates the umami flavor.
